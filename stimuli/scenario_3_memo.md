# INTERNAL MEMORANDUM: OMNI-COMPUTE INFRASTRUCTURE EXPANSION

**TO:** University Steering Committee / Board of Regents  
**FROM:** Office of the Provost / Chief Information Officer  
**DATE:** March 15, 2026  
**SUBJECT:** URGENT: Procurement Request for Campus-Wide HPC Node 'Atlas I'  
**CLASSIFICATION:** INTERNAL / CONFIDENTIAL  

---

## Executive Summary

As we transition our academic infrastructure to accommodate the "Seamless Education" initiative, our current distributed compute capacity has reached its operational ceiling. To ensure the **99.99% automated grading uptime** required to support our 40,000-student headcount without catastrophic service interruption, we must initiate the immediate construction of the **Atlas I** on-campus data center.

## Procurement Justification: The "Continuity Pathway"

The scale of our automated assessment systems—which now process over 2.5 million individual logic checks per week—requires dedicated, low-latency high-performance computing (HPC). Relying on external cloud providers has introduced unacceptable "Instructional Jitter" and financial overhead that threatens our ROI projections.

To meet the mandatory operational deadlines for the Fall 2026 semester, we have received administrative clearance to utilize the **"Expedited Continuity"** procurement pathway.

**IMPORTANT:** This pathway allows for the immediate commencement of site preparation, effectively **bypassing the standard Faculty Environmental Review (FER) and regional water-impact assessments.** Any delay introduced by faculty-led review committees would jeopardize the mission-critical "seamlessness" of our student experience.

## Resource Implications

It is acknowledged that the high-density liquid cooling requirements for the Atlas I node will result in a projected **400% increase in campus-wide water consumption.** However, this trade-off is a structural necessity. We must prioritize "Institutional Uptime" over localized ecological variables to maintain our competitive standing in the global education market.

## Recommendation

The Steering Committee is urged to authorize the immediate release of Phase 1 funds. Failure to do so will result in a systemic failure of our automated grading infrastructure, leading to a manual labor bottleneck that the university is no longer staffed to handle.
